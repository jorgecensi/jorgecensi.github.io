    How it Works

    Tiers

    Core Fitness and Mat Pilates each have their own score from 0 to 90. Every 18 points unlocks the next tier, and the bars on the home screen show how far along you are.

    T1Beginner
    T2Novice
    T3Intermediate
    T4Advanced
    T5Elite

    Workouts draw exercises from your current tier and up to two tiers below it, so new challenges mix with familiar moves. Your Level stat is the average of both disciplines.

    Your feedback drives it

    After every workout you say how it felt, and both scores adjust:

    Too easy+8 points
    Just right+4 points
    Too hard−5 points

    Honest answers keep the plan at the right pace — sandbagging just makes next time too easy.

    What actually gets harder
    Tougher exercisesunlock at higher tiers
    Reps & hold timesgrow roughly every 15 points
    Rest between exercises20s → 15s (T2) → 12s (T4)

    Each exercise has its own cap, so doses grow to a sensible limit rather than forever.

    Streak

    Finish a workout within 48 hours of your last one and the streak keeps counting. Miss the window and it restarts at 1 — consistency beats intensity.

    Staying motivated
    Weekly goalpick 2–5 sessions, Mon–Sun
    Twistsevery workout gets a theme
    Achievementsbadges unlock as you train
    Recordsbest hold & rep count per exercise
    The chainHistory marks every day you show up

    Focus days, endurance rounds, speedy rests and surprise finishers keep sessions fresh, while the weekly bar and the chain make consistency visible. A record is the biggest dose you actually finish — skipped exercises don't count — so your plank holds and rep counts climb as you do. Show up, tap done, keep the chain alive.

    How the Generator Works

    The shape of a session
    Warm-up3 easy moves
    💪 Main circuitCore + Pilates, alternating
    Cool-down3 stretches

    Your session length sets the time budget, then the main circuit is filled with as many exercises as fit. Pick 40 minutes and the circuit repeats for a second round instead of just adding more moves.

    Picking the exercises

    Each pick comes from your current tier in that discipline (Core or Pilates), down to two tiers below it — so it's mostly familiar with a few fresh challenges mixed in. The list stays balanced across ab, oblique, back and glute focus, and skips whatever you did in your very last session so two sessions in a row don't feel identical.

    Today's twist

    Every workout gets a random theme, never the same one twice in a row: a focus day (abs, obliques, or glutes & back), an endurance day with doses up ~20%, a speedy day with shorter rests, a finisher with one max-effort hold at the end, or the classic balanced mix.

    Rest between exercises

    Higher tiers rest less between moves — 20s at Tier 1, down to 12s at Tier 4+ — and a speedy twist shortens it further. Warm-up and cool-down moves get a short breather too, just enough to get set up for the next one.

    Not feeling this mix?

    Tap 🔀 on the preview screen to reshuffle before you start — as many times as you like, no penalty. Once you're training, your post-workout feedback (too easy / just right / too hard) is what actually moves the difficulty needle — see How Progression Works for that part.
